who are we?

Klitmøller is an old fishing village by the North Sea on the West Coast of Denmark.The fishermen have been fishing from the open coast for centuries and are still doing it to this day.

the sea of the past and present

Fishing
&
Surfing

for people drawn by raw Nordic nature

Life on the sea demands warm clothes and the properties of wool made it the perfect material for the fishermen on the boats. Furthermore sheep was one of the only animals able to survive in the windblown landscape surrounding the small village. Meaning the fishing families could make their own clothing for the rough weather and life on the open coast. Since the 1980 ́s it has also been a very popular destination for surfers from the North of Europe and now attracts thousands of surfers every year. Both cultures evolve around the sea and create a uniques mix of old and new aspects.

ethics

We believe we have a responsibility as a brand. This goes for both the environment and for the people that are part of the process making what you wear. We work with a set of ethics in three major areas:

the materials.

We use sustainable materials whenever possible. This means we always strive to use very high quality organic cotton, linen and natural fibres such as merino and lambswool for our garments, that makes them last for as long a possible. Being sustainable is not a destination, but a journey. Step by step. And we feel we are on the right path.

the production.

We currently produce in Portugal and Germany at smaller, mostly family owned, factories, that we visit on a regular basis. The factories have a high standard in workmanship, ethics and the workers earn a fair pay. The regulations for waste materials are also more strict in Europe, than in most other parts of the world. After all – it´s all the same planet.

the packaging.

We are on a mission to deliver all our products in recycled paper bags and cartons. We have started this process and aim to pack and deliver in packaging that leave as little impact as possible. It´s a process of closing the loop. Buy wisely – the money you spend today will affect the world you live in tomorrow.
